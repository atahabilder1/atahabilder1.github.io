About Me

I am a Ph.D. Candidate in the Department of Computer Science at Wayne State University, conducting research in the Big Data Research Lab under the mentorship of Professor Dr. Shiyong Lu. My research lies at the intersection of Blockchain Security, Smart Contract Auditing, and Machine Learning for Vulnerability Detection. I am passionate about developing intelligent systems that leverage AI to enhance the security and reliability of decentralized technologies. I also have a strong interest in Machine Learning and Data Engineering, building scalable data pipelines and applying ML techniques to solve real-world problems.

Research

ActorAI - Smart Contract Auditing Framework

Big Data Research Lab | Sep 2021 - Present

Leading an AI-powered smart contract auditing framework analyzing Solidity codebases for vulnerabilities (reentrancy, access control flaws, integer overflows). Built automated audit pipeline processing 500+ professional security reports with ML-based detection models.

Applied deep learning (PyTorch, Transformers) achieving 85%+ accuracy in vulnerability classification. Utilized static analysis (ASTs, CFGs) with Tree-sitter and Slither for semantic feature engineering.

PyTorch Transformers Slither Solidity

BGPSentry - Blockchain-based BGP Security

NIDS Lab | Sep 2024 - Present

Architecting a permissioned blockchain system for securing internet routing infrastructure. Designed custom consensus mechanism and smart contracts for BGP announcement logging.

Developed cryptographic proof-of-announcement protocols and token-based staking mechanisms. Built AS-level network graphs and anomaly detectors using NetworkX and scikit-learn.

Blockchain NetworkX Cryptography Python
